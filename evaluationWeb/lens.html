Why pursuing a degree at the College of William and Mary?

 

·         William and Mary moved up a spot to 32nd overall in national rankings and the College maintained its spot as the sixth-best public university in the country, according to the 2009 U.S. News & World Report.

·         Founded in 1693, William and Mary is the second oldest educational institution in the U.S. Although Harvard began operation first, William and Mary’s antecedents actually predate those of the Massachusetts institution.

·         Four US Presidents benefited from educational programs offered by the College: George Washington, Thomas Jefferson, James Monroe and John Tyler.

·         The College's student/faculty ratio of 11/1 is the lowest among the top public universities--and comparable to those at the nation's top 25 schools. In addition, nearly 50 percent of William and Mary's classes have fewer than 20 students.

More reasons can be found at http://www.wm.edu/about/wmbynumbers/
